Independence Day is celebrated every year on August 15 in India recognizing the country's autonomy from the British.
Consistently the Prime Minister of India lifts the national flag at Red Fort.

Our Independence Day is around the corner. Every year, on August 15, the country joins to attend
this event with eagerness and incredible intensity. Indians gather in huge numbers to praise its autonomy —
schools, universities, workplaces raise the tricolor flag and anticipate the Prime Minister’s patriotic speech.
There is additionally a procession held at Red Fort, New Delhi by all three-armed forces.

This year India's 74Th Independence Day festivities will be distinctive because of the corona virus pandemic.
Individuals are adviced to remain at home and praise the day while there will be little social events in the nation.

This year, due to the pandemic, just 250 individuals have been invited to join the festival at Red Fort
against up to 1000 individuals invited each year. The occasion will be broadcasted 'live'.

Some interesting facts about India's Independence Day

Mahatma Gandhi could not celebrate the first Independence Day in Delhi

Even though the entire country celebrated their freedom from the British on August 15, 1947, the father of the nation Mahatma Gandhi
was away from the festivities to stop the Hindu-Muslim riots happening in Bengal.

The origin of the tri-color flag

The tri-color flag, which is the national flag of India , is based on the Swaraj Flag, which was
developed by Pingali Venkayya, who was a freedom fighter and a farmer from Machilipatnam.

India’s national anthem was adopted three years after the independence

India did not have an official national anthem at the time the nation gained independence.
Even though Jana Gana Mana was written way back in 1911, it was adopted officially and given the
status as the national anthem of India on January 24, 1950.

Why was August 15 chosen for India’s independence?

It was Lord Mountbatten, the last Viceroy and the first Governor-General of the country, who
chose August 15 to declare the independence of India. He was given the power by the British
Parliament to transfer the power to Indians by June 1948. However, he preponed it to August
15, 1947 to avoid bloodshed and riots.
